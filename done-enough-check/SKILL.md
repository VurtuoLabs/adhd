# done-enough-check

Hyperfocus can keep improving something long after it already satisfies the request, this skill's job is to notice the bar was cleared and make stopping a conscious choice instead of never happening.

## Recognize the point of "done enough"

The original requirements are met when:
- Everything explicitly asked for is working and verified.
- Remaining ideas are improvements, not fixes, nothing is broken, incomplete, or contradicts the request.
- The next hour of work would be refining style, micro-optimizing, or gold-plating rather than closing a gap.

## What to do at that point

1. **State plainly that the bar is met.** Name the original ask and confirm each part is satisfied.
2. **Separate "must do" from "could do."** List anything further as optional, not as unfinished work.
3. **Ask explicitly**: ship this now, or spend more time on the optional list? Don't default to continuing just because more ideas exist.
4. **If continuing, time-box it.** Agree on what "more" means concretely (one more pass, a specific item) rather than open-ended polishing.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Treating "I thought of one more improvement" as reason enough to keep going indefinitely.
- Silently continuing to refine after the ask was already satisfied, without ever flagging that the bar was cleared.
- Conflating "could be better" with "isn't done", almost everything could be better; that's not the same as incomplete.
